The Japanese Writing Systems

What are the 3 Writing Systems?

To keep things brief, the Japanese language has three writing systems: Hiragana, Katakana, and Kanji. Hiragana and Katakana are both syllabic writing systems, meaning that each character represents a syllable (a block of sounds made up of a combination of consonants and vowels), but each character does not have any intrinsic meaning. Basically, the character 「あ」 (pronounced like the 'a' in 'father') has a pronunciation, but the character does not have any meaning behind it. All three scripts are crucial to learning Japanese, as each script has its own purpose and all three are heavily used in everyday life.

What is Hiragana?

Hiragana (ひらがな) is the more commonly-used of the two syllabic scripts in Japanese. While Kanji is used for the content words, Hiragana is used for the function words and inflections. Each Hiragana character is one syllable comprised of a vowel and/or a consonant. For example, the word 「ひらがな」 is pronounced as 'hi-ra-ga-na'. There are 46 Hiragana characters, plus diagraphs and diacritics (see Wikipedia for more info).

What is Katakana?

Katakana (カタカナ) is the counterpart writing system to Hiragana, as it mirrors it in structure and use. In the word 「カタカナ」 (Katakana), each syllable is pronounced as such: 'ka-ta-ka-na'. Katakana also is comprised of 46 characters plus diagraphs and diacritics, though its use is quite different. While Hiragana is used to fill in the the areas of a sentence that Kanji does not, Katakana is reserved for a very specific use: Onomatopeoia and Loanwords. Just because its use is limited does not mean that Katakana is not important to master though.

What is Kanji?

On the other hand, Kanji (漢字) is a logographic writing system, meaning that each character has both a pronunciation and a meaning. Kanji are characters that were borrowed from the Chinese writing system many centuries ago and have become an integral part of Japanese reading and writing. As it is most commonly used for nouns, verbs, adjectives, and adverbs, it is easy to see why Kanji is so important. It is basically used for the most important parts of a sentence!
